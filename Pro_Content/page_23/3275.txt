<div class="plm"><table align="center"><tr><td><b>Time Limit:</b> 2000MS</td><td width="10px"></td><td><b>Memory Limit:</b> 65536K</td></tr><tr><td><b>Total Submissions:</b> 2861</td><td width="10px"></td><td><b>Accepted:</b> 1352</td></table></div><p class="pst">Description</p><div class="ptx" lang="en-US"><p>Each of Farmer John's <i>N</i> cows (1 &le; <i>N</i> &le; 1,000) produces milk at a different positive rate, and FJ would like to order his cows according to these rates from the fastest milk producer to the slowest.</p><p>FJ has already compared the milk output rate for <i>M</i> (1 &le; <i>M</i> &le; 10,000) pairs of cows. He wants to make a list of <i>C</i> additional pairs of cows such that, if he now compares those <i>C</i> pairs, he will definitely be able to deduce the correct ordering of all <i>N</i> cows. Please help him determine the minimum value of <i>C</i> for which such a list is possible.</p></div><p class="pst">Input</p><div class="ptx" lang="en-US">Line 1: Two space-separated integers: <i>N</i> and <i>M</i><br>Lines 2..<i>M</i>+1: Two space-separated integers, respectively: <i>X</i> and <i>Y</i>. Both <i>X</i> and <i>Y</i> are in the range 1...<i>N</i> and describe a comparison where cow <i>X</i> was ranked higher than cow <i>Y</i>.</div><p class="pst">Output</p><div class="ptx" lang="en-US">Line 1: A single integer that is the minimum value of <i>C</i>.</div><p class="pst">Sample Input</p><pre class="sio">5 5
2 1
1 5
2 3
1 4
3 4</pre><p class="pst">Sample Output</p><pre class="sio">3</pre><p class="pst">Hint</p><div class="ptx" lang="en-US">From the information in the 5 test results, Farmer John knows that since cow 2 > cow 1 > cow 5 and cow 2 > cow 3 > cow 4, cow 2 has the highest rank. However, he needs to know whether cow 1 > cow 3 to determine the cow with the second highest rank. Also, he will need one more question to determine the ordering between cow 4 and cow 5. After that, he will need to know if cow 5 > cow 3 if cow 1 has higher rank than cow 3. He will have to ask three questions in order to be sure he has the rankings: "Is cow 1 > cow 3?  Is cow 4 > cow 5? Is cow 5 > cow 3?"</div><p class="pst">Source</p><div class="ptx" lang="en-US"><a href="searchproblem?field=source&key=USACO+2007+March+Gold">USACO 2007 March Gold</a></div></td></tr></table>
