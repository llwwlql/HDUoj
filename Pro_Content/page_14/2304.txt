<div class="plm"><table align="center"><tr><td><b>Time Limit:</b> 1000MS</td><td width="10px"></td><td><b>Memory Limit:</b> 65536K</td></tr><tr><td><b>Total Submissions:</b> 4674</td><td width="10px"></td><td><b>Accepted:</b> 2883</td></table></div><p class="pst">Description</p><div class="ptx" lang="en-US"><img align=right src=images/2304_1.gif>Now that you're back to school for another term, you need to remember how to work the combination lock on your locker. A common design is that of the Master Brand, shown at right. The lock has a dial with 40 calibration marks numbered 0 to 39. A combination consists of 3 of these numbers; for example: 15-25-8. To open the lock, the following steps are taken: <br><ul><li>turn the dial clockwise 2 full turns <br><li>stop at the first number of the combination <br><li>turn the dial counter-clockwise 1 full turn <br><li>continue turning counter-clockwise until the 2nd number is reached <br><li>turn the dial clockwise again until the 3rd number is reached <br><li>pull the shank and the lock will open. </ul><br>Given the initial position of the dial and the combination for the lock, how many degrees is the dial rotated in total (clockwise plus counter-clockwise) in opening the lock? </div><p class="pst">Input</p><div class="ptx" lang="en-US">Input consists of several test cases. For each case there is a line of input containing 4 numbers between 0 and 39. The first number is the position of the dial. The next three numbers are the combination. Consecutive numbers in the combination will be distinct. A line containing 0 0 0 0 follows the last case. </div><p class="pst">Output</p><div class="ptx" lang="en-US">For each case, print a line with a single integer: the number of degrees that the dial must be turned to open the lock.</div><p class="pst">Sample Input</p><pre class="sio">0 30 0 30
5 35 5 35
0 20 0 20
7 27 7 27
0 10 0 10
9 19 9 19
0 0 0 0
</pre><p class="pst">Sample Output</p><pre class="sio">1350
1350
1620
1620
1890
1890
</pre><p class="pst">Source</p><div class="ptx" lang="en-US"><a href="searchproblem?field=source&key=Waterloo+local+2003.09.20">Waterloo local 2003.09.20</a></div></td></tr></table>
