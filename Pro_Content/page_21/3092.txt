<div style='position: absolute; right: 10px;'>Language:<select size=1 onChange="window.location.href='problem?id=3092&lang='+this.value+'&change=true'"><option value='default' selected>Default</option></select></div><div class="ptt" lang="en-US">Non-divisible 2-3 Power Sums</div>
<div class="plm"><table align="center"><tr><td><b>Time Limit:</b> 1000MS</td><td width="10px"></td><td colspan="3"><b>Memory Limit:</b> 65536K</td></tr><tr><td><b>Total Submissions:</b> 1039</td><td width="10px"></td><td><b>Accepted:</b> 506</td><td width="10px"></td><td style="font-weight:bold; color:red;">Special Judge</td></tr></table></div><p class="pst">Description</p><div class="ptx" lang="en-US"><p>Every positive integer <i><b>N</b></i> can be written in at least one way as a sum of terms of the form (2<sup>a</sup>)(3<sup>b</sup>) where no term in the sum exactly divides any other term in the sum. For example:</p><blockquote><p>1 = (2<sup>0</sup>)(3<sup>0</sup>)<br>7 = (2<sup>2</sup>)(3<sup>0</sup>) + (2<sup>0</sup>)(3<sup>1</sup>)<br>31 = (2<sup>4</sup>)(3<sup>0</sup>) + (2<sup>0</sup>)(3<sup>2</sup>) + (2<sup>1</sup>)(3<sup>1</sup>) = (2<sup>2</sup>) + (3<sup>3</sup>)</p></blockquote><p>Note from the example of 31 that the representation is not unique.</p><p>Write a program which takes as input a positive integer <i><b>N</b></i> and outputs a representation of <i><b>N</b></i> as a sum of terms of the form (2<sup>a</sup>)(3<sup>b</sup>).</p></div><p class="pst">Input</p><div class="ptx" lang="en-US"><span lang="en-us"><p>The first line of input contains a single integer <i><b>C</b></i> (1 &le; <i><b>C</b></i> &le; 1000) which is the number of datasets that follow.</p><p>Each dataset consists of a single line of input containing a single integer <i><b>N</b></i> (1 &le; <i><b>N</b></i> &lt; 2<sup>31</sup>), which is the number to be represented as a sum of terms of the form (2<sup>a</sup>)(3<sup>b</sup>).</p></span></div><p class="pst">Output</p><div class="ptx" lang="en-US"><p>For each dataset, the output will be a single line consisting of: The dataset number, a single space, the number of terms in your sum as a decimal integer followed by a single space followed by representations of the terms in the form <code>[&lt;2 exponent&gt;,&lt;3 exponent&gt;]</code> with terms separated by a single space. <code>&lt;2 exponent&gt;</code> is the power of 2 in the term and <code>&lt;3 exponent&gt;</code> is the power of 3 in the term.</p></div><p class="pst">Sample Input</p><pre class="sio">6
1
7
31
7776
531441
123456789</pre><p class="pst">Sample Output</p><pre class="sio">1 1 [0,0]
2 2 [2,0] [0,1]
3 3 [4,0] [0,2] [1,1]
4 1 [5,5]
5 1 [0,12]
6 8 [3,13] [4,12] [2,15] [7,8] [9,6] [0,16] [10,5] [15,2]</pre><p class="pst">Source</p><div class="ptx" lang="en-US"><a href="searchproblem?field=source&key=Greater+New+York+2006">Greater New York 2006</a></div></td></tr></table>
