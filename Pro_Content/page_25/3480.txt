There is one big box filled with M&amp;Ms of different colors. At first John has to eat several M&amp;Ms of the same color. Then his opponent has to make a turn. And so on. Please note that each player has to eat at least one M&amp;M during his turn. If John (or his brother) will eat the last M&amp;M from the box he will be considered as a looser and he will have to buy a new candy box.</p><p>Both of players are using optimal game strategy. Next line will contain <b>N</b> integers <b>A<sub>i</sub></b>, separated by spaces &ndash; amount of M&amp;Ms of <b>i</b>-th color.</p><p><b>Constraints:<br>1 &lt;= T &lt;= 474,<br>1 &lt;= N &lt;= 47,<br>1 &lt;= A<sub>i</sub> &lt;= 4747</b></p></div><p class="pst">Output</p><div class="ptx" lang="en-US"><p>Output <b>T</b> lines each of them containing information about game winner.
