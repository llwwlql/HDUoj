<div class="plm"><table align="center"><tr><td><b>Time Limit:</b> 1000MS</td><td width="10px"></td><td><b>Memory Limit:</b> 65536K</td></tr><tr><td><b>Total Submissions:</b> 1378</td><td width="10px"></td><td><b>Accepted:</b> 750</td></table></div><p class="pst">Description</p><div class="ptx" lang="en-US"><p>Following the account of Jules Verne, a scrambled message written by the middle age alchemist Arne Saknussemm, and deciphered by professor Lidenbrock, started the incredible travel to the center of the Earth. The scrambling procedure used by Arne is alike the procedure given below.</p><p>1. Take a non empty message M that contains letters from the English alphabet, digits, commas, dots, quotes (i.e. <tt>'</tt>), spaces and line breaks, and whose last character is different than space. For example, consider the following message whose translation reads "In Sneffels's crater descend brave traveler, and touch the center of the Earth".</p><table border="0" align="center"><tr><td><pre>In Sneffels craterem descende audas<br>viator, et terrestre centrum attinges.</pre></td></tr></table>2. Choose an integral number <i>0&lt;K&le;length(M)</i> and add trailing spaces to <i>M</i> such that the length of the resulting message, say <i>M'</i>, is the least multiple of <i>K</i>. For <i>K=19</i> and the message above, where <i>length(M)=74</i> (including the <i>8</i> spaces and the line break that <i>M</i> contains), two trailing spaces are added yielding the message <i>M'</i> with <i>length(M')=76</i>.</p><p>3. Replace all the spaces from <i>M'</i> by the character <tt>_</tt> (underscore) ; replace all the line breaks from <i>M'</i> by <tt>\</tt> (backslash), and then reverse the message. In our case:</p><table border="0" align="center"><tr><td><pre>__.segnitta_murtnec_ertserret_te_,rotaiv\sadua_ednecsed_meretarc_sleffenS_nI</pre></td></tr></table><p>4. Write the message that results from step 3 in a table with <i>length(M')/K</i> rows and <i>K</i> columns. The writing is column wise.
