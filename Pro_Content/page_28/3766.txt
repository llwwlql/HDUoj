<div class="plm"><table align="center"><tr><td><b>Time Limit:</b> 1000MS</td><td width="10px"></td><td><b>Memory Limit:</b> 65536K</td></tr><tr><td><b>Total Submissions:</b> 416</td><td width="10px"></td><td><b>Accepted:</b> 128</td></table></div><p class="pst">Description</p><div class="ptx" lang="en-US"><div><p>Hexagon Coin Toss is a simple game played on a Hexagon chessboard. Players toss a coin on the chessboard and see how many hexagons intersect with the coin. Now you are given a task to calculate the result of the coin toss----for each different number of intersecting hexagons, just output the probability.</p><p>To simplify the problem, we assume that the side length of each hexagon is 1 and the radius of the coin will not exceed 0.5 (so that the coin can cover at most 3 hexagons).</p><p><img src="images/3766_2.gif"></p><p>The chessboard contains several rows of hexagons and numbers of hexagons in all odd-number-row are the same (That is true for all even-number-rows). The chessboard is described as (<i>N</i>, <i>M</i>, <i>K</i>). Here <i>N</i> representing the number of hexagons in the longest row. M representing the number of rows and <i>K</i> shows the number of hexagons in the first row. So the chessboard above can be represented as (4, 3, 3).</p><p>The center of the coin will be in the chessboard and we do not take anything outside the chessboard into consideration. In the situation below, the coin covers 2 hexagons.</p><p><img border="0" src="images/3766_3.gif"></p></div></div><p class="pst">Input</p><div class="ptx" lang="en-US"><div><p>The input contains multiple test cases.<br />The first line of each case contains three integers <i>N</i>, <i>M</i>, <i>K</i>, representing the size of the chessboard. <i>N</i> and <i>M</i> will be in the range of [1ï¼Œ1000] and <i>K</i> equals either <i>N</i> or <i>N</i>-1. It is guaranteed that all input information is valid (So that (2, 1, 1) will not appear).<br />The following line is a real number <i>R</i>, the radius of the coin.<br />Input is ended with a case of <i>N</i>=<i>M</i>=<i>K</i>=0.</p></div></div><p class="pst">Output</p><div class="ptx" lang="en-US"><div><p>For each test case, output the case number first.<br />The following 3 lines are the result information, as shown in the sample output. The result should be rounded to 0.001.<br />Print an empty line after each test case.</p></div></div><p class="pst">Sample Input</p><pre class="sio">4 5 4
0.38
4 5 3
0.26
4 2 3
0.24
0 0 0
</pre><p class="pst">Sample Output</p><pre class="sio">Case 1:
Probability of covering 1 hexagon   = 48.303 percent.
Probability of covering 2 hexagons  = 31.300 percent.
Probability of covering 3 hexagons  = 20.397 percent.

Case 2:

Probability of covering 2 hexagons  = 27.934 percent.
Probability of covering 3 hexagons  = 10.110 percent.

Case 3:
Probability of covering 1 hexagon   = 72.550 percent.
Probability of covering 2 hexagons  = 22.220 percent.
Probability of covering 3 hexagons  = 5.230 percent.
