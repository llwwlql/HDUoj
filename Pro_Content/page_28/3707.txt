<div class="plm"><table align="center"><tr><td><b>Time Limit:</b> 10000MS</td><td width="10px"></td><td colspan="3"><b>Memory Limit:</b> 65536K</td></tr><tr><td><b>Total Submissions:</b> 345</td><td width="10px"></td><td><b>Accepted:</b> 21</td><td width="10px"></td><td style="font-weight:bold; color:red;">Special Judge</td></tr></table></div><p class="pst">Description</p><div class="ptx" lang="en-US"><div><p>Let &lt; <i>p</i><sub>1</sub>, <i>p</i><sub>2</sub>,..., <i>p<sub>n</sub></i> &gt; and &lt; <i>q</i><sub>1</sub>, <i>q</i><sub>2</sub>,..., <i>q<sub>n</sub></i> &gt; be two sequences of vectors in the m-dimensional Euclidean space. Is that possible to transform the former using a series of reflections and/or rotations so that it becomes the latter? </p></div></div><p class="pst">Input</p><div class="ptx" lang="en-US"><div><p>The input consists of a single data set given in the format (<i>n, m</i> &le; Blank characters are irrevelant. </div></div><p class="pst">Output</p><div class="ptx" lang="en-US"><div><p>It is known that both reflections and rotations are linear transformations, which are represented by squarematrices. In particular, in the <i>m</i>-dimensional Euclidean space, they are represented by m × m matrices. Therefore, if the desire sequence of reflections and rotations exists, you are to output a matrix <i><b>H</b></i> in the format </p><br><center><img src="formula?tex=%5Cbegin%7Btabular%7D%7Bcccc%7D+%5Ctextit%7Bm%7D+%26+%26+%26+%5C%5C+%5Ctextit%7Bh%7D%24_%7B11%7D%24+%26+%5Ctextit%7Bh%7D%24_%7B12%7D%24+%26+%24%5Ccdots%24+%26+%5Ctextit%7Bh%7D%24_%7B1m%7D%24+%5C%5C+%5Ctextit%7Bh%7D%24_%7B21%7D%24+%26+%5Ctextit%7Bh%7D%24_%7B22%7D%24+%26+%24%5Ccdots%24+%26+%5Ctextit%7Bh%7D%24_%7B2m%7D%24+%5C%5C+%24%5Cvdots%24+%26+%24%5Cvdots%24+%26+%24%5Cddots%24+%26+%24%5Cvdots%24+%5C%5C+%5Ctextit%7Bh%7D%24_%7Bm1%7D%24+%26+%5Ctextit%7Bh%7D%24_%7Bm2%7D%24+%26+%24%5Ccdots%24+%26+%5Ctextit%7Bh%7D%24_%7Bmm%7D%24+%5C%5C+%5Cend%7Btabular%7D+" alt="\begin{tabular}{cccc} \textit{m} &#38; &#38; &#38; \\ \textit{h}$_{11}$ &#38; \textit{h}$_{12}$ &#38; $\cdots$ &#38; \textit{h}$_{1m}$ \\ \textit{h}$_{21}$ &#38; \textit{h}$_{22}$ &#38; $\cdots$ &#38; \textit{h}$_{2m}$ \\ $\vdots$ &#38; $\vdots$ &#38; $\ddots$ &#38; $\vdots$ \\ \textit{h}$_{m1}$ &#38; \textit{h}$_{m2}$ &#38; $\cdots$ &#38; \textit{h}$_{mm}$ \\ \end{tabular} " align="absmiddle"></center><br><p>such that <i><b>Hp</b><sub>i</sub></i> = <i><b>q</b><sub>i</sub></i> for all 1≤i≤n; otherwise, you only have to output an asterisk (&quot;*&quot;).
