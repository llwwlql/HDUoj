<div class="plm"><table align="center"><tr><td><b>Time Limit:</b> 1000MS</td><td width="10px"></td><td><b>Memory Limit:</b> 65536K</td></tr><tr><td><b>Total Submissions:</b> 5204</td><td width="10px"></td><td><b>Accepted:</b> 2721</td></table></div><p class="pst">Description</p><div class="ptx" lang="en-US">FJ has purchased N (1 <= N <= 2000) yummy treats for the cows who get money for giving vast amounts of milk. FJ sells one treat per day and wants to maximize the money he receives over a given period time.<br><br>The treats are interesting for many reasons:<ul><li>The treats are numbered 1..N and stored sequentially in single file in a long box that is open at both ends. On any day, FJ can retrieve one treat from either end of his stash of treats.</li><li>Like fine wines and delicious cheeses, the treats improve with age and command greater prices.</li><li>The treats are not uniform: some are better and have higher intrinsic value. Treat i has value v(i) (1 <= v(i) <= 1000).</li><li>Cows pay more for treats that have aged longer: a cow will pay v(i)*a for a treat of age a.</li></ul>Given the values v(i) of each of the treats lined up in order of the index i in their box, what is the greatest value FJ can receive for them if he orders their sale optimally?<br><br>The first treat is sold on day 1 and has age a=1. Each subsequent day increases the age by 1.</div><p class="pst">Input</p><div class="ptx" lang="en-US">Line 1: A single integer, N<br><br>Lines 2..N+1: Line i+1 contains the value of treat v(i)</div><p class="pst">Output</p><div class="ptx" lang="en-US">Line 1: The maximum revenue FJ can achieve by selling the treats</div><p class="pst">Sample Input</p><pre class="sio">5
1
3
1
5
2</pre><p class="pst">Sample Output</p><pre class="sio">43</pre><p class="pst">Hint</p><div class="ptx" lang="en-US">Explanation of the sample:<br><br>Five treats. On the first day FJ can sell either treat #1 (value 1) or treat #5 (value 2).<br><br>FJ sells the treats (values 1, 3, 1, 5, 2) in the following order of indices: 1, 5, 2, 3, 4, making 1x1 + 2x2 + 3x3 + 4x1 + 5x5 = 43.</div><p class="pst">Source</p><div class="ptx" lang="en-US"><a href="searchproblem?field=source&key=USACO+2006+February+Gold+%26+Silver">USACO 2006 February Gold & Silver</a></div></td></tr></table>
