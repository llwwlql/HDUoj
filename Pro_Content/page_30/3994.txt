<div class="plm"><table align="center"><tr><td><b>Time Limit:</b> 1000MS</td><td width="10px"></td><td><b>Memory Limit:</b> 65536K</td></tr><tr><td><b>Total Submissions:</b> 1741</td><td width="10px"></td><td><b>Accepted:</b> 1209</td></table></div><p class="pst">Description</p><div class="ptx" lang="en-US">Number guessing is a popular game between elementary-school kids. Teachers encourage pupils to play the game as it enhances their arithmetic skills, logical thinking, and following-up simple procedures. We think that, most probably, you too will master in few minutes. Here’s one example of how you too can play this game: Ask a friend to think of a number, let’s call it n<sub>0</sub>. Then:<br><ol><br><li>Ask your friend to compute n<sub>1</sub> = 3 * n<sub>0</sub> and to tell you if n<sub>1</sub> is even or odd.</li><br><li>If n<sub>1</sub> is even, ask your friend to compute n<sub>2</sub> = n<sub>1</sub>/2. If, otherwise, n<sub>1</sub> was odd then let your friend compute n<sub>2</sub> = (n<sub>1</sub> + 1)/2.</li><br><li>Now ask your friend to calculate n<sub>3</sub> = 3 * n<sub>2</sub>.</li><br><li>Ask your friend to tell tell you the result of n<sub>4</sub> = n<sub>3</sub>/9. (n<sub>4</sub> is the quotient of the division operation. In computer lingo, ’/’ is the integer-division operator.)</li><br><li>Now you can simply reveal the original number by calculating n<sub>0</sub> = 2 * n<sub>4</sub> if n<sub>1</sub> was even, or n<sub>0</sub> = 2 * n<sub>4</sub> + 1 otherwise.</li><br></ol><br>Here’s an example that you can follow: If n<sub>0</sub> = 37, then n<sub>1</sub> = 111 which is odd. Now we can calculate n<sub>2</sub> = 56, n<sub>3</sub> = 168, and n<sub>4</sub> = 18, which is what your friend will tell you. Doing the calculation 2 * n<sub>4</sub> + 1 = 37 reveals n<sub>0</sub>.</div><p class="pst">Input</p><div class="ptx" lang="en-US">Your program will be tested on one or more test cases. Each test case is made of a single positive number (0 &lt; n<sub>0</sub> &lt; 1,000,000).<br>The last line of the input file has a single zero (which is not part of the test cases.)</div><p class="pst">Output</p><div class="ptx" lang="en-US">For each test case, print the following line:<br>k. B Q<br>Where k is the test case number (starting at one,) B is either ’even’ or ’odd’ (without the quotes) depending on your friend’s answer in step 1. Q is your friend’s answer to step 4.</div><p class="pst">Sample Input</p><pre class="sio">37
38
0</pre><p class="pst">Sample Output</p><pre class="sio">1. odd 18
2. even 19</pre><p class="pst">Source</p><div class="ptx" lang="en-US"><a href="searchproblem?field=source&key=anarc+2009">anarc 2009</a></div></td></tr></table>
