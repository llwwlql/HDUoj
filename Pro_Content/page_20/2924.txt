<div class="plm"><table align="center"><tr><td><b>Time Limit:</b> 1000MS</td><td width="10px"></td><td><b>Memory Limit:</b> 65536K</td></tr><tr><td><b>Total Submissions:</b> 9791</td><td width="10px"></td><td><b>Accepted:</b> 4349</td></table></div><p class="pst">Description</p><div class="ptx" lang="en-US"><span lang="en-us"><p>Johann Carl Friedrich Gau&szlig; (1777 &ndash; 1855) was one of the most important German mathematicians. For those of you who remember the Deutsche Mark, a picture of him was printed on the 10 &ndash; DM bill. In elementary school, his teacher J. G. B&uuml;ttner tried to occupy the pupils by making them add up the integers from 1 to 100. The young Gau&szlig; surprised everybody by producing the correct answers (5050) within seconds.</p><p>Can you write a computer program that can compute such sums really quickly?</p><p>Given two integers <i>n</i> and <i>m</i>, you should compute the sum of all the integers from <i>n</i> to <i>m</i>. In other words, you should compute</p></span><center><img src=images/2924_1.gif></center></div><p class="pst">Input</p><div class="ptx" lang="en-US"><span lang="en-us"><p>The first line contains the number of scenarios. Then print the sum of all integers from <i>n</i> to <i>m</i>. Terminate the output for the scenario with a blank line.</p></span></div><p class="pst">Sample Input</p><pre class="sio">3
