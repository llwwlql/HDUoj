<div class="plm"><table align="center"><tr><td><b>Time Limit:</b> 1000MS</td><td width="10px"></td><td><b>Memory Limit:</b> 10000K</td></tr><tr><td><b>Total Submissions:</b> 996</td><td width="10px"></td><td><b>Accepted:</b> 446</td></table></div><p class="pst">Description</p><div class="ptx" lang="en-US">Four Quarters is a game of chance played with, well, four quarters. Two people, called A and B, each flip two quarters each round. They each gain or lose points each round based on the following tables:<br><center><img src=images/1217_1.jpg></center><br>There is no difference between Heads/Tails and Tails/Heads. As you can see, the odds are stacked in Player A's favor. At the beginning of the game, each player has 0 points, and points accumulate as the game progresses. At the<br>end of the game, whichever player has the most points wins.<br>You must write a program that determines the probability that Player A will win, Player B will win, or they will tie, after a certain number of rounds. Assume that the coins are fair, i.e. that heads and tails are equally likely.<br></div><p class="pst">Input</p><div class="ptx" lang="en-US">There is no input file for this problem.</div><p class="pst">Output</p><div class="ptx" lang="en-US">Output a table that lists the probability that Player A will win, B will win, or they will tie, after each round for 1 to 20 rounds.
