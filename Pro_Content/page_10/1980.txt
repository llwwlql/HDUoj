<div class="plm"><table align="center"><tr><td><b>Time Limit:</b> 1000MS</td><td width="10px"></td><td><b>Memory Limit:</b> 30000K</td></tr><tr><td><b>Total Submissions:</b> 4455</td><td width="10px"></td><td><b>Accepted:</b> 1747</td></table></div><p class="pst">Description</p><div class="ptx" lang="en-US">A fraction whose numerator is 1 and whose denominator is a positive integer is called a unit fraction. A representation of a positive rational number p/q as the sum of finitely many unit fractions is called a partition of p/q into unit fractions. For example, 1/2 + 1/6 is a partition of 2/3 into unit fractions. The difference in the order of addition is disregarded. For example, we do not distinguish 1/6 + 1/2 from 1/2 + 1/6. <br><br>For given four positive integers p, q, a, and n, count the number of partitions of p/q into unit fractions satisfying the following two conditions. <br><br>The partition is the sum of at most n many unit fractions. <br>The product of the denominators of the unit fractions in the partition is less than or equal to a. <br>For example, if (p,q,a,n) = (2,3,120,3), you should report 4 since <br><center><img src=images/1980_1.jpg></center><br>enumerates all of the valid partitions. <br></div><p class="pst">Input</p><div class="ptx" lang="en-US">The input is a sequence of at most 200 data sets followed by a terminator. <br><br>A data set is a line containing four positive integers p, q, a, and n satisfying p,q <= 800, a <= 12000 and n <= 7. The integers are separated by a space. <br><br>The terminator is composed of just one line which contains four zeros separated by a space. It is not a part of the input data but a mark for the end of the input. <br></div><p class="pst">Output</p><div class="ptx" lang="en-US">The output should be composed of lines each of which contains a single integer. No other characters should appear in the output. <br><br>The output integer corresponding to a data set p, q, a, n should be the number of all partitions of p/q into at most n many unit fractions such that the product of the denominators of the unit fractions is less than or equal to a. <br></div><p class="pst">Sample Input</p><pre class="sio">2 3 120 3
2 3 300 3
2 3 299 3
2 3 12 3
2 3 12000 7
54 795 12000 7
2 3 300 1
2 1 200 5
2 4 54 2
0 0 0 0
</pre><p class="pst">Sample Output</p><pre class="sio">4
7

2
