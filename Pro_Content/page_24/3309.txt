<div class="plm"><table align="center"><tr><td><b>Time Limit:</b> 2000MS</td><td width="10px"></td><td><b>Memory Limit:</b> 65536K</td></tr><tr><td><b>Total Submissions:</b> 646</td><td width="10px"></td><td><b>Accepted:</b> 174</td></table></div><p class="pst">Description</p><div class="ptx" lang="en-US"><p>The great Lucky Luke and his smart horse Jolly Jumper are again caught the four evil Dalton brothers and sent them to the prison. As a reward, the governor of the province in which the Daltons had been caught, gave a collection of gold stones to Luke. At first, Luke became very happy for obtaining such a treasure, but he remembered he had a limited space to carry these stones. In fact, he can just count on the kyack on the back of Jolly which consists of two bags with volume <i>V</i> on either side of Jollyâ€™s packsaddle. As a result, he cannot carry all the stones and must take just some of them. </p><p>He thought, &quot;At least, I can use as much space as possible in the Jolly's kyack by taking a portion of some of the stones with myself.&quot; But Unlucky Luke found that all smiths, instead of their wage for cutting the portion of a stone that he wants, will take the left portion of the stone (for example, if Luke gives the smith one stone and asks him to cut and give him 20% of it, the smith will take the remaining 80% as his wage). What an unfair deal! They know Luke is rich now and will not change their mind! Now, Luke wants to select a portion of each stone in a way that he could place them in two bags in his kyack without exceeding their volume and the selected stones have the maximum total value. Unfortunately, Luke that fires a gun faster than his shadow (see the picture below for the proof of this claim!) can not solve this problem and needs your help! It should be noted that a portion with <i>p</i>% of a stone volume has <i>p</i>% of its value.</p></div><p class="pst">Input</p><div class="ptx" lang="en-US"><p>Input begins with a number <i>T</i> showing the number of test cases and then, <i>T</i> test cases follow. Each test case begins with a line containing an integer 1 &le; <i>n</i> &le;100 and a positive real number <i>V</i> &le; 5000 representing the number of stones and volume of each bag respectively. After that, a line containing <i>n</i> positive integers less than or equal to 100 will come where the <i>i</i>th number shows the volume of <i>i</i>th gold stone. Then, a line containing <i>n</i> positive real numbers will come where the <i>i</i>th number shows the value of <i>i</i>th gold stone.</p></div><p class="pst">Output</p><div class="ptx" lang="en-US"><p>For each test case, your program must output the maximum total value of stones which can be carried by Luke rounded to four digits after the decimal point.</p></div><p class="pst">Sample Input</p><pre class="sio">1
4 10.0
6 7 8 9
10.0 12.0 14.0 16.0</pre><p class="pst">Sample Output</p><pre class="sio">35.0952</pre><p class="pst">Source</p><div class="ptx" lang="en-US"><a href="searchproblem?field=source&key=Amirkabir+University+of+Technology+Local+Contest+2006">Amirkabir University of Technology Local Contest 2006</a></div></td></tr></table>
