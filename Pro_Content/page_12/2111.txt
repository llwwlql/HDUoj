<div class="plm"><table align="center"><tr><td><b>Time Limit:</b> 2000MS</td><td width="10px"></td><td><b>Memory Limit:</b> 30000K</td></tr><tr><td><b>Total Submissions:</b> 2035</td><td width="10px"></td><td><b>Accepted:</b> 764</td></tr><tr><td colspan="3" align="center" style="color:#0ead00"><b>Case Time Limit:</b> 1000MS</td></tr></table></div><p class="pst">Description</p><div class="ptx" lang="en-US">The cows have revised their game of leapcow.  They now play in the middle of a huge pasture upon which they have marked a grid that bears a remarkable resemblance to a chessboard of N rows and N columns (3 <= N <= 365).<br><br>Here's how they set up the board for the new leapcow game:<br><br>* First, the cows obtain N x N squares of paper.  They write the integers from 1 through N x N, one number on each piece of paper.<br><br>* Second, the 'number cow' places the papers on the N x N squares in an order of her choosing.<br><br>Each of the remaining cows then tries to maximize her score in the game.<br><br>* First, she chooses a starting square and notes its number.<br><br>* Then, she makes a 'knight' move (like the knight on a chess board) to a square with a higher number.  If she's particularly strong, she leaps to the that square; otherwise she walks.<br><br>* She continues to make 'knight' moves to higher numbered squares until no more moves are possible.<br><br>Each square visited by the 'knight' earns the competitor a single point.  The cow with the most points wins the game.<br><br>Help the cows figure out the best possible way to play the game.</div><p class="pst">Input</p><div class="ptx" lang="en-US">* Line 1: A single integer: the size of the board<br><br>* Lines 2.. ...: These lines contain space-separated integers that tell the contents of the chessboard.  The first set of lines (starting at the second line of the input file) represents the first row on the chessboard; the next set of lines represents the next row, and so on.  To keep the input lines of reasonable length, when N > 15, a row is broken into successive lines of 15 numbers and a potentially shorter line to finish up a row.  Each new row begins on its own line.<br></div><p class="pst">Output</p><div class="ptx" lang="en-US">* Line 1: A single integer that is the winning cow's score; call it W.<br><br>* Lines 2..W+1: Output, one per line, the integers that are the starting square, the next square the winning cow visits, and so on through the last square. If a winning cow can choose more than one path, show the path that would be the 'smallest' if the paths were sorted by comparing their respective 'square numbers'.<br></div><p class="pst">Sample Input</p><pre class="sio">4
1 3 2 16
4 10 6 7
8 11 5 12
9 13 14 15</pre><p class="pst">Sample Output</p><pre class="sio">7
2
4
5
9
10
12
13
