<div class="plm"><table align="center"><tr><td><b>Time Limit:</b> 1000MS</td><td width="10px"></td><td><b>Memory Limit:</b> 10000K</td></tr><tr><td><b>Total Submissions:</b> 643</td><td width="10px"></td><td><b>Accepted:</b> 231</td></table></div><p class="pst">Description</p><div class="ptx" lang="en-US">You are responsible for ordering a large pizza for you and your friends. Each of them has told you what he wants on a pizza and what he does not; of course they all understand that since there is only going to be one pizza, no one is likely to have all their requirements satisfied. Can you order a pizza that will satisfy at least one request from all your friends? <br><br>The pizza parlor you are calling offers the following pizza toppings; you can include or omit any of them in a pizza: <br><pre><br>Input Code     Topping <br>A              Anchovies <br>B              Black Olives <br>C              Canadian Bacon <br>D              Diced Garlic <br>E              Extra Cheese <br>F              Fresh Broccoli <br>G              Green Peppers <br>H              Ham <br>I              Italian Sausage <br>J              Jalapeno Peppers <br>K              Kielbasa <br>L              Lean Ground Beef <br>M              Mushrooms <br>N              Nonfat Feta Cheese <br>O              Onions <br>P              Pepperoni </pre><br>Your friends provide you with a line of text that describes their pizza preferences. For example, the line <br><br>+O-H+P; <br><br>reveals that someone will accept a pizza with onion, or without ham, or with pepperoni, and the line <br><br>-E-I-D+A+J; <br><br>indicates that someone else will accept a pizza that omits extra cheese, or Italian sausage, or diced garlic, or that includes anchovies or jalapenos. <br><br></div><p class="pst">Input</p><div class="ptx" lang="en-US">The input consists of a series of pizza constraints. <br>A pizza constraint is a list of 1 to 12 topping constraint lists each on a line by itself followed by a period on a line by itself. <br>A topping constraint list is a series of topping requests terminated by a single semicolon. <br>An topping request is a sign character (+/-) and then an uppercase letter from A to P. </div><p class="pst">Output</p><div class="ptx" lang="en-US">For each pizza constraint, provide a description of a pizza that satisfies it. A description is the string "Toppings: " in columns 1 through 10 and then a series of letters, in alphabetical order, listing the toppings on the pizza. So, a pizza with onion, anchovies, fresh broccoli and Canadian bacon would be described by: <br><br>Toppings: ACFO <br><br>If no combination toppings can be found which satisfies at least one request of every person, your program should print the string <br>"No pizza can satisfy these requests." on a line by itself starting in column 1. </div><p class="pst">Sample Input</p><pre class="sio">+A+B+C+D-E-F-G-H;
-A-B+C+D-E-F+G+H;
-A+B-C+D-E+F-G+H;
.

+O+M+L;
-O-P;
+M-L+P;
.</pre><p class="pst">Sample Output</p><pre class="sio">Toppings: 
Toppings: CELP 
No pizza can satisfy these requests. </pre><p class="pst">Source</p><div class="ptx" lang="en-US"><a href="searchproblem?field=source&key=South+Central+USA+1997">South Central USA 1997</a></div></td></tr></table>
